Career

Gayl became the director of the Ostpreussische Landgesellschaft, an East Prussian settlement society, in 1909.
He served in the First World War as an active-duty officer and was awarded the Iron Cross first class, but he quickly joined the Ober Ost administration as Supreme Commander of All German Forces in the East. In 1916, he was appointed Chief of the Ober Ost Department of Interior Politics and Administration, and on September 1, 1918, he was appointed Landeshauptmann (“State Captain”) of northern Lithuania in Kaunas.
Gayl was a part of the German delegation at the Versailles conference in 1919, and in 1920, he was appointed German Commissioner for the Allenstein Plebiscite precinct [de] during the East Prussian plebiscite.
