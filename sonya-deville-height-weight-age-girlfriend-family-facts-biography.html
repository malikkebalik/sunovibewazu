    First WWE Match

    In December 2015, she made her in-ring debut in a match against Nia Jax at the NXT live event.

    First MMA Match

    In March 2015, she made her MMA debut in a match against Jasmine Pouncy at the University of MMA. She lost the match via a unanimous decision.

    First Film

    In 2013, she made her theatrical film debut in a lead role in the crime drama movie, The Honour.

    First TV Show

    In 2012, Sonya Deville made her first TV show appearance in Tattoo Nightmares.

    Personal Trainer

    Sonya Deville regularly works out at the WWE Performance Center under the watchful eyes of Sean Hayes. Sean focuses on improving her core strength and lower body strength. Apart from getting her to do the big traditional lifts such as squats and deadlifts, Hayes has included functional training exercises in her workout.

    They use props such as suspension bands and resistance bands for her workout. They also use sleds to add more value to the workout.

    Sonya Deville Facts

  • In June 2015, she managed to reach the finals of the 6th season of Tough Enough, a WWE competition. However, she was the 3rd competitor to be eliminated from the event.
  • In October 2015, she was signed up by WWE and was assigned to the developmental division, NXT of the promotion.
  • She started using her popular ring name Sonya Deville in May 2017. Her first match with the new ring name was a battle royal for NXT Women’s Championship.
  • She has served as a co-presenter of the popular YouTube show, UFC AfterBuzz.
  • She is very open about her sexual orientation, which has made her the first openly lesbian WWE performer.
  • Sonya has been trained by Lita, Booker T, and Billy Gunn.
  • She started taking MMA and fighting training at the age of 17. However, when she had first turned up for MMA training, her trainer believed that being a girl, she won’t be able to last in his classes.
