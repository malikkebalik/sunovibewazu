Is Bo Horvat Playing Tonight? Injury And Health Update 2023

Is Bo Horvat Playing Tonight? has been asked by his fans as he got injured while playing Hockey last Saturday.

 Bowie William Horvat, a Canadian ice hockey centre and captain of the National Hockey League (NHL) Vancouver Canucks, was born on April 5, 1995. In the NHL Entry Draft of 2013, he was chosen ninth overall.

 He won two J. Ross Robertson Cups with the London Knights and the 99th Wayne Gretzky Award as the most outstanding player in the 2013 playoffs during his junior career in the Ontario Hockey League (OHL).

 He represented Team Canada internationally in the 2012 IIHF U20 Championship, the 2012 World U-17 Hockey Challenge, and the 2012 Ivan Hlinka Memorial Tournament (under-18).

Is Bo Horvat Playing Tonight?

No, Bo Horvat isn’t playing tonight; he’s making his first trip back to Vancouver after playing for the Canucks for a portion of his first nine seasons (from 2019–20 to 2022-23).

Regarding goals (8th, 201) and points (10th, 420) in franchise history, Horvat is ranked in the top 10.

In the first year of his new eight-year contract, Horvat has nine points and four goals, ranking him as the Islanders’ second-leading scorer thus far this season.

On November 7, the Islanders did not run lines during practice. Oliver Wahlstrom is anticipated to play on the team’s top line, and Mathew Barzal will shift back to center if Horvat is sidelined versus Minnesota.

If Horvat were to lose for any time, New York would likely see further line adjustments.

Horvat’s injury kept him out of the game, but after participating in line rushes during warm-ups, it appears he’s well. With his return to the starting lineup, the 23-year-old should get some time on the first power-play unit.

Bo Horvat Injury Details

Bo Horvat, the first-line center for the New York Islanders, did not practice on Monday morning following what seemed to be a left ankle injury sustained during the team’s loss to the Carolina Hurricanes on Saturday night.

Head coach Lane Lambert gave him the day-to-day status, and a new assessment will be conducted on Tuesday before their game against the Minnesota Wild at UBS Arena.

The 28-year-old was hit by a Brady Skjei slapshot off the area midway through the third period, which resulted in the injury. Despite going through the tunnel to be examined, he was able to come back to the game.

With 9:33 remaining in the third, Bo Horvat departed the rink after deflecting a Brady Skjei shot off his left ankle.

At first, it appeared the puck had hit Horvat’s left skate, shattering the blade. However, that was not what emerged out of Horvat’s foot.

Horvat did make a comeback in just five minutes, contributing to three more games throughout regulation and one during OT.

Bo Horvat Health Update 2023

With Horvat’s injury, a list confined to the blue line gains a notable forward.

After being shaken up on Saturday, Scott Mayfield, who missed seven games due to a foot ailment, did not skate on Monday either. Lambert explained that he was away for a maintenance day.

Despite his mid-body injury sustained against the Washington Capitals on Thursday, Adam Pelech could return to the ice.

Regarding Pelech, Lambert stated, “I thought he was OK.” “After practice, we’ll see how he is going forward.”
