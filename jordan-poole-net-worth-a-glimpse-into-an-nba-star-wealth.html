Jordan Poole’s College Career

Poole was a four-star recruit out of high school, according to ESPN. He earned scholarship offers from many collegiate basketball schools. Virginia Tech, Nebraska, Memphis, Marquette, Indiana, Illinois, Auburn, and Michigan were among them. Poole would eventually commit to Michigan.

Poole spent two seasons with the Michigan Wolverines. He averaged 9.4 points, 2.2 rebounds, and 1.4 assists per game over two seasons while shooting 37% from beyond the arc. Poole hit a game-winning three-pointer in the second round of the 2018 NCAA Tournament during his rookie year, helping the Wolverines beat Houston 64-63. Poole aided Michigan to another Sweet 16 berth in his second season.

Jordan Poole’s Professional Career

Poole declared for the 2019 NBA Draft after two seasons with the Michigan Wolverines, forgoing his NCAA eligibility. Poole was picked 28th overall by the Golden State Warriors on draft night. Spotrac reports he then signed a $10 million, four-year rookie contract.

Poole largely played off the bench in his NBA debut. He averaged 8.8 points, 2.1 rebounds, and 2.4 assists in restricted minutes. Poole averaged 12.0 points, 1.8 rebounds, and 1.9 assists a season later.

Poole also played for the Golden State Warriors’ G-League club, the Santa Cruz Warriors. Poole averaged 26.0 points, 6.0 rebounds, and 5.3 assists in the minor league. The first-round selection made the All-NBA G-League Third Team.

The Wolverines star had his greatest season in 2021-22. He averaged 18.5 points, 3.4 rebounds, and 4.0 assists while shooting a career-best 36% from 3-point range. Better still, Poole helped Stephen Curry and the Warriors win the NBA title. Poole made 38.5 percent of his 3-pointers in the NBA Finals against the Boston Celtics, second only to Stephen Curry.
