All About Iman Shumpert | Net Worth, Relationship, Career

Iman Asante Shumpert, better known as Iman Shumpert, is a well-known American basketball player. He has played for the Brooklyn Nets of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He began his career in 2011 as the 17th overall pick in the NBA draft by the New York Knicks.

What is the Net Worth of Iman Shumpert? Salary, Earnings

In 2023, Shumpert’s net worth is estimated to be $40 million. This is what he has learned through playing professional basketball. He has numerous contracts with various teams, many of which have paid him well. He is also a rap music singer, which has contributed to his wealth.

Where was Iman Shumpert born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Iman Shumpert was born on June 26, 1990, in Berwyn, Illinois, in the United States. He is the son of Odis, an insurance salesman, and L’Tanya, an art and design adjunct professor at Columbia College. He looks to be an only child because he has not given any information about his siblings.

Shumpert graduated with honors from Oak Park and River Forest High Schools. He was a first-team all-state selection and one of the country’s top 30 seniors. Iman led his team to three conference titles and was named conference MVP during his junior and senior years. He enrolled at Georgia Tech and was the school’s fourth-leading scorer as a freshman in 2008-09.

In the 2009-10 season, he missed six games following arthroscopic surgery to repair an ailing meniscus. He led his team in scoring with 17.3 points per game in the 2010-11 season, becoming the seventh player in ACC history to do so. He holds the Georgia Tech record for most steals per game. Iman declared for the NBA draft on March 28, 2011, foregoing his last year of NCAA eligibility.

How did Iman Shumpert start his Professional Career?

Shumpert made his NBA debut in 2011 after being chosen as the 17th overall pick in the draft. At the time, he was a member of the New York Knicks. He tore his ACL in a first-round playoff game against the Miami Heat, forcing him to miss the remainder of the season. He finished fifth in the 2012 NBA Rookie of the Year voting with 33 votes. Iman was medically cleared to practice with the squad on January 13, 2013.

He made his season debut against the Detroit Pistons on January 17th at London’s O2 Arena. He dislocated his left shoulder on December 12, 2014, and was forced to leave the team for three weeks. The Cleveland Cavaliers traded him on January 25, 2015.

He signed a four-year contract with the Cavaliers on July 9, 2015. On September 29, 2015, he was sidelined for three months after hurting his left wrist. He made his season debut against the Orlando Magic on December 11th. He was ruled out for 6-8 weeks after surgery on his left knee on December 1, 2017. On January 23, 2018, he returned to the court to face the San Antonio Spurs.

He was traded to the Sacramento Kings during the 2018-2019 season. In 2019, he was acquired by the Huston Rockets. Wilson Chandler joined with the Brooklyn Nets on November 13, 2019, following his suspension. Chandler’s suspension was lifted on December 12th, and the Nets waived Shumpert. He inked a contract to return to the Brooklyn Nets on January 30, 2021, but he was waived after one game. He signed a new 10-day deal with the Nets on February 29.
