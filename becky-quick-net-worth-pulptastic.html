What is Becky Quick’s Net Worth and Salary?

Becky Quick, an American television news and business personality, has a net worth of $20 million dollars, combined with her husband, CNBC producer Matt Quayle. However, Quick is the primary source of the wealth.

She is best known for co-anchoring CNBC’s financial news shows, “Squawk Box” and “On the Money.” Before joining CNBC, she wrote for the Wall Street Journal and helped launch the publication’s website in 1996.

Throughout her career, Quick has interviewed numerous influential investors and power brokers, including Warren Buffett, Charlie Munger, and Alan Greenspan.

Early Life and Education

Becky Quick was born on July 18, 1972 in Gary, Indiana. She moved frequently due to her father’s work as a geologist, living in states such as Ohio, Oklahoma, and Texas. Eventually, her family settled in Medford, New Jersey. Quick attended Rutgers University in New Brunswick, where she served as editor-in-chief of the Daily Targum. She received the Times Mirror Fellowship from Rutger’s Journalism Resources Institute and graduated in 1993 with a bachelor of arts degree in political science.

Quick’s Career at Wall Street Journal

After graduating from college, Quick wrote for the Wall Street Journal, covering e-commerce and retail industries. She also played a significant role in launching the publication’s website in 1996 and subsequently became the international news editor. As an editor, Quick supervised coverage of foreign affairs.

Becky Quick’s Career on “Squawk Box”

In 2005, Quick co-hosted the financial news program “Squawk Box” on CNBC alongside Joe Kernen and Carl Quintanilla, later joined by Andrew Ross Sorkin. The daily program covers breaking news and analysis related to the financial markets, as well as featuring banter between the hosts and guests. Regular contributors include Guy Adami, Ron Insana, Rick Santelli, Steve Liesman, and Sharon Epperson.

After being hosted from New Jersey for nearly two decades, “Squawk Box” moved to a new studio in New York City’s Time-Life Building in early 2015. Two years later, it moved again to the NASDAQ MarketSite studio in Times Square.

Quick’s Hosting Stint on “On the Money”

Becky Quick held another major position on CNBC as the host of “On the Money,” a syndicated weekly show that premiered in 1970 under the title “The Wall Street Journal Report.” The program covered a range of economic topics, including job reports, stock market updates, and interviews with business and political figures. In 2013, the show was renamed “On the Money with Maria Bartiromo,” with Quick becoming the new host later that year when Bartiromo left. The program ultimately ended production in December of 2019. During her time as host, Quick interviewed notable guests including Colin Powell and Henry Paulson.

Becky Quick’s Career Highlights

Becky Quick has interviewed some of the most prominent business power brokers and investors on the planet, including Charlie Munger, Warren Buffett, Bill Gates, T. Boone Pickens, Alan Greenspan, and Jamie Dimon. She has also interviewed three presidents of the United States.

Quick hosted the television documentary “Warren Buffett: The Billionaire Next Door Going Global” in 2007. Later, in October of 2015, she served as a co-moderator at one of the Republican presidential debates.

Quick has hosted panels at many of the most renowned conferences in the world, including Fortune’s Most Powerful Women’s Conference; the Microsoft CEO Conference; and the Allen & Co. Sun Valley Media Conference. Meanwhile, she serves on the board of the Economic Club of New York, a non-profit membership organization focused on the promotion and discussion of economic, social, and political issues.

Becky Quick’s Marriages and Family

Becky Quick was first married to computer programmer Peter Shay from 2006 to 2008. She later married Matt Quayle, the co-creator and executive producer of “Squawk Box,” whom she met at Rutgers University. They have two children together, a son named Kyle and a daughter named Kaylie. Quayle also has two daughters from a prior marriage, Kimiko and Natalie. The family resides in New Jersey.

Becky Quick’s Salary at CNBC

According to sources, CNBC anchor Becky Quick earns a salary of $3 million dollars per year.
