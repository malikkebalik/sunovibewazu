Conway The Machine Uses 50 Cent As Inspiration To Walk On Injured Leg

Conway The Machine has managed to muster up the strength to take a few light steps on his dislocated knee with some inspiration from 50 Cent.

Earlier this week, the Buffalo rapper uploaded a video of himself standing upright in the hospital and walking with the assistance of a walker and the nurses on hand. Stitched into the video was the chorus from 50 Cent’s 2003 Get Rich Or Die Tryin‘ album cut “Gotta Make It To Heaven.”

“I gotta make it to Heaven for going through Hell/ I gotta make it to Heaven, I hope I make it to Heaven,” 50 Cent raps over the infectious beat, speaking to Conway’s desire to overcome going through the “hell” of breaking his leg and dislocating his knee.

Conway The Machine previously announced that he had to postpone his forthcoming Won’t He Do It Tour due to his leg injury. The unfortunate news was delivered to fans in an Instagram post on Monday (May 22).

“The results are in,” he wrote with a facepalm emoji. “I have a Fractured right tibia, dislocated kneecap, ANNNNND the album of the year tho!”

AD LOADING...

He continued: “But In all seriousness tho, anyone that knows me knows there’s nothing I love to do more than bein up on that stage! I can’t lie I’m super disappointed that I have to postpone the WHDi tour for a little bit, because I was really looking forward to goin on those stages and rocking this WHDI shit for y’all live. However I am in good spirits and I’m only looking at it as just a minor lil setback for a MAJOR COMEBACK! No stress!!”

“If u already bought tickets they’ll be honored at the later dates for sure! More updates coming this week. I appreciate all the love and concern for my well-being, love y’all.”

AD LOADING...

The tour was in promotion of his recent album of the same name, which dropped earlier this month. The gritty LP features assists from Westside Gunn, Benny The Butcher, Sauce Walka, Fabolous, Ransom, Juicy J, Dave East and more.

On the production side, there are beats from Daringer, J.U.S.T.I.C.E. League, Juicy J and Daniel Cruz, among others.
